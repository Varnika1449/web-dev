space

Space is an immense, mostly empty, three-dimensional expanse where objects and events have relative position and direction; it's a vacuum with almost no matter and extremely low pressure, where sound doesn't travel. Here's a more detailed look at space: What is Space? Definition: Space is the area beyond the upper limits of Earth's atmosphere, a boundless three-dimensional extent where objects and events have relative position and direction. Vacuum: Space is a vacuum, meaning it's almost completely empty, with extremely low pressure and almost no matter. No Sound: Because of the lack of molecules, sound cannot travel through space. Expansion: The universe is observed to be 13.8 billion years old and has been expanding since its formation in the Big Bang. Key Concepts: Galaxies: Our solar system, including the Sun and planets, is part of a galaxy called the Milky Way.
